Welcome to about us

Muhle’s Safe Haven was born out of a deep calling to stand up for those whose voices have been silenced by abuse and fear. We are a non-profit organization that exists to protect, uplift, and walk beside survivors of Gender-Based Violence (GBV). Our name, Muhle, meaning “beautiful” in isiZulu, reflects our core belief: that every survivor holds beauty, strength, and worth, no matter what they’ve been through. We are more than just a shelter; we are a beacon of hope, a supportive family, and a catalyst for transformation. Our Story Founded by survivors, allies, and mental health professionals, Muhle's Safe Haven began as a grassroots effort in response to the growing need for safe, trauma-informed spaces for GBV victims in our community. We saw the pain. We heard the silence. And we knew we had to act. What started as a small initiative has grown into a comprehensive support center where women, children, and LGBTQ+ survivors can find healing, justice, and empowerment. Our Mission To create a safe, inclusive, and empowering environment for all survivors of gender-based violence by providing holistic support services, advocacy, and education. Our Vision A world free from GBV, where survivors are heard, respected, and restored—emotionally, physically, and socially. Our Values Compassion: We lead with empathy and understanding. Confidentiality: Your story is yours—we hold it with care and discretion. Empowerment: We believe in your strength, even when you don’t. Equality: We serve all survivors, regardless of gender, race, religion, or identity. Community: Healing doesn’t happen alone—we’re stronger together.
